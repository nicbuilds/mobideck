Premium Species

Scientifically proven durability. Artistically selected grains. Discover the properties of our world-class timber.

Cedro (Cedar)

Highly valued for its natural **thujaplicins**—chemical compounds that serve as natural fungicides. It is exceptionally dimensionally stable, meaning it resists warping, twisting, and checking even when exposed to fluctuating humidity.

Janka Hardness: 900 lbf
Best For: Deck Railing & Pergolas

Maculí

Scientifically known as *Tabebuia rosea*, this wood is remarkably resistant to fungal decay and termites. It features a fine to medium texture with a straight to irregular grain, offering a high-luster finish that is ideal for luxury exterior surfaces.

Durability: Very High
Origin: Tropical America

Tzalam

Often called "Mayan Walnut," Tzalam is one of the most sought-after tropical hardwoods. It has a high density and natural luster. Its heartwood is highly resistant to rot and moisture, making it superior for heavy-traffic outdoor decks.

Janka Hardness: 1,400 lbf
Color: Deep Chocolate/Plum

Teca (Teak)

The silica content in Teak makes it virtually waterproof. It contains a high percentage of natural oils that act as a barrier against water, preventing rot and brittleness even without constant sealing.

Silica Content: High (Self-Protecting)
Status: Elite Maritime Grade

Parota

Parota (*Enterolobium cyclocarpum*) is unique for its extreme weight-to-strength ratio. It is significantly lighter than other hardwoods, which reduces structural load while providing massive, wide-plank aesthetics with striking golden-brown sapwood edges.

Grain: Interlocked
Eco Rating: Sustainable/Fast Growing
